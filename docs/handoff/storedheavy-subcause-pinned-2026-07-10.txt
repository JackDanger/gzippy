PINNED SUB-CAUSE (Gate-2, both arches NOT yet replicated — AMD-only so far,
see OWED below): worker per-iteration latency in `decode_marker_fast_loop`
transfers ~1:1 into the storedheavy wall — CONFIRMS (not hypothesis) that
per-thread marker-decode throughput is the critical-path lever, matching the
phasebreak's decode_wait-dominant attribution. HOWEVER the H-vs-M split as
designed CANNOT further localize compute vs bandwidth: Arm H and Arm M are
serially ADJACENT in the same single-threaded, non-pipelined per-iteration
loop body (compute -> store -> next iteration's compute, no overlap between
the two halves within one thread at this granularity), so a delay inserted
at EITHER point adds identically to the same serial critical chain — of
course they respond identically; this is an algebraic property of the loop's
structure, not new information about which sub-op is naturally slower in the
UNPERTURBED loop. This mechanism was NOT one of the brief's three anticipated
outcomes (H>>M / M>>H / both flat) — it is a fourth outcome ("both strongly
AND EQUALLY proportional") that the brief's decision tree did not enumerate;
reporting it as such rather than forcing it into "compute-bound" or
"bandwidth-bound" is the disciplined call per the governing anti-bias law.

RECOMMENDED LEVER (single, per the brief): reduce TOTAL per-iteration
serial-chain latency in `decode_marker_fast_loop`'s hot body — the fungible
combination of litlen decode/consume + the widened u16 ring store, since
Step-2 proves ANY reduction anywhere in that per-iteration chain transfers
~1:1 to wall. Concretely this points at software-pipelining /
overlapping the store of iteration N with the decode of iteration N+1 (so the
two currently-serial halves gain slack from each other, changing the
"identical H vs M response" property this experiment measured), OR at
straight instruction-count reduction in either half (CLAUDE.md-open
territory: BMI2/table/refill-cadence work on the compute half, narrower/
batched stores on the write half). A follow-up perturbation that could
further localize the split: inject the SAME total delay but spread across
MULTIPLE non-adjacent iterations' compute vs store (so pipelining slack, if
architecturally added, could differentially absorb one arm) — not attempted
this session (would require the pipelining lever to exist first to be
testable).
