## pigz & zlib-ng are DOMINATED (not separate fronts)

- **pigz:** strictly the slowest single-stream decoder at T1 on BOTH arches (x86: 2–3×
  the frontier; aarch64: behind libdeflate). pigz `-d` does **NOT** parallelize inflate
  (x86 silesia pigz T1=1769 ≈ T2=1962 ≈ T4=1723 — flat with T). gz's BEST config beats
  pigz's BEST on every corpus/arch (e.g. x86 monorepo gz-T1=153 < pigz-any-T≈312–367;
  aarch64 nasa gz-T4=101 < pigz-any≈98–139, gz-T1=109≈pigz). **Beating the frontier
  auto-beats pigz.**
- **zlib-ng:** dominated by the frontier on every corpus/arch (igzip on x86, libdeflate
  on aarch64) → not a separate front. (Caveat for honesty: zlib-ng is stronger on x86
  than field knowledge says — it beats libdeflate on 2/4 x86 corpora — but it never
  beats igzip.)

**Net:** the surpass list collapses from {libdeflate, igzip, zlib-ng, pigz, rapidgzip}
to **ONE frontier per arch** (igzip x86 / libdeflate aarch64) plus **hold rapidgzip at
T≥2**. zlib-ng and pigz fall out as dominated.

## T≥2 (parallel) — gz vs rapidgzip & pigz (NOTE the pinning artifact)

- **gz ≈ rapidgzip at T≥2 on x86** (gz/rg ratio: silesia T2 1.002 / T4 1.041; monorepo
  T2 1.003 / T4 0.941; nasa T2 0.935 / T4 0.871; squishy T2 0.974 / T4 1.027) — TIE-to-
  winning, consistent with the MATRIX-COMPLETE rg parity status. Same on aarch64 (gz
  beats rg at every T — rg has no arm ISA-L tail).
- **gz parallel beats pigz at T4 on every corpus, both arches** (e.g. x86 silesia
  gz-T4=514 vs pigz-T4=1723 = 3.4×). pigz does not scale.
- ⚠️ **PINNING ARTIFACT (do not bank the T2/T4 ABSOLUTE walls):** on the Intel
  even-P-core pin (mask 4 / 4,6 / 4,6,8,10) BOTH gz AND rapidgzip are ~slower at T2
  than at T1 (e.g. gz silesia T1=822 → T2=1405). This hits gz and rg *equally* (ratio
  stays ~1.0) so the gz-vs-rg comparison is valid, but the T2 absolutes are a
  pin/scheduling artifact of this rig and are NOT the production parallel wall — the
  `standing.sh` rig (un-pinned pool) is the authority for parallel absolutes. T1
  single-stream (the frontier axis) is unaffected and clean (CV<1%).
