# DIS-25 gate — is (B) heterogeneous-core work-distribution the bankable, DIS-6-distinct high-T lever?

Independent disproof advisor (read-only, Opus). Verified source first-hand. Verdict: **(B) is sound as a DIAGNOSIS (high-T loss is NOT dominantly engine-W; gz under-feeds E-cores), but the NAMED LEVER is NOT distinct from DIS-6 — it re-discovers the same consumer-lag / lean-consumer divergence, names two fix surfaces already refuted (prefetch-depth) or unfaithful (out-of-order publish), and rests its one novel sub-claim (finer partitioning) on a non-comparable metric. Do NOT open it as a fresh fix. Run DIS-6's still-owed serial-work-vs-decode-wait decompose first.**

## Per-claim

### Q1 — (B)-not-(A), roofline soundness → SOUND (with one tautology caveat)
- (A) "engine-W amplified on E-cores" is REFUTED as the DOMINANT cause. P2's per-core gz/rg ratio is flat (0.898 P vs 0.879 E); the ~10-12% engine deficit is uniform across core types. The high-T loss (6-23%) is far larger than the 1.9pp E-vs-P difference, so per-core E-amplification cannot be the driver. SOUND.
- "gz WINS T8 (1.026)" is real and bankable (== DIS-24 T8 1.001). But **effGZ 0.350 > effRG 0.306 proves nothing beyond the wall**: efficiency = wall/(N·rate); gz's single-core roofline rate is ~10% lower (205.8 vs 229.3), so for the same wall gz's efficiency is mechanically ~10% higher. The "engine masked at T8" claim reduces to "gz's 8-P wall ties/beats rg" — true, but don't quote effGZ>effRG as an independent fact; it is the wall deficit re-expressed.
- "rg extracts ~2x from added E-cores" (wall drop 67ms vs 35ms, T8→T16): this is a DIRECT wall measurement (not a model artifact) and is internally apples-to-apples (DIS-25 ran the whole sweep at the expanded cores:24). Directionally SOUND. Caveat: the "% captured of roofline" framing inherits the -P1-pinned-rate assumption; trust the raw wall-delta, not the captured-fraction.

### Q2 — DIS-24 confound + bankability → FIX-NEEDED (provenance) / loss is BANKABLE
- The LXC guest 199 is Proxmox `cores:16` ⇒ cpuset 0-15 = P-core SMT only; E-cores 16-23 excluded. `taskset -c 16` ERRORS ("Invalid argument") — it does NOT silently remap. So DIS-24's E-core cells (T9-E…T16-Ephys, T24, T32) either (a) ran with an unrecorded container expansion or (b) failed. Their provenance is UNVERIFIED. **DIS-24's E-cell numbers must not be quoted independently.**
- DIS-25 supersedes them (verified expansion to cores:24, restored after). The SMT-only cells were always valid under cores:16: **T16-SMT 0.912 (DIS-24, valid) and T16-Ephys 0.934 (DIS-25, verified-expansion) both LOSE.** The high-T LOSS is bankable from two independent provenances. SOUND.

### Q3 — distinctness from DIS-6 → REFUTED (the named lever is a re-tread)
The lever names three sub-components; checked each against source + the DIS-6 record:
- **prefetch-DEPTH:** REFUTED already. `prefetch-horizon-findings.md` + advisor: depth is vendor-identical byte-for-byte — `m_cache = max(16,P)`, `m_prefetchCache = 2P` (chunk_fetcher.rs:520-529 ↔ BlockFetcher.hpp:181-183), ramp/pump identical. DIS-6: "prefetching MORE of the WRONG (guess) offsets does nothing." Deeper prefetch is a closed door.
- **out-of-order publish:** UNFAITHFUL. rg's consumer is **strictly in-order**: ParallelGzipReader.hpp:575-628 `while((nBytesDecoded<nBytesToRead)&&!eof()){ blockResult = chunkFetcher().get(m_currentPosition); … m_currentPosition += nBytesToDecode; }`. rg does NOT publish output out of order; window resolution is inherently serial (window N feeds N+1) in both tools. rg is not the existence proof for "out-of-order publish" — so this is not a faithful lever.
- **heterogeneous work-distribution / "rg partitions finer (66 vs 34)":** rests on a NON-COMPARABLE metric. gz "chunks" = `finish_decode` (distinct finished partitions). rg "Total Fetched" = `prefetchCount + onDemandFetchCount` = fetch OPERATIONS, inflated by re-fetches of evicted chunks (BlockFetcher.hpp Statistics, lines 111-112). Both tools port the IDENTICAL chunk-size formula (single_member.rs:71-94 ↔ ParallelGzipReader.hpp:294-306), which would yield the SAME partition count for the same file+P — so a 34-vs-66 gap means the two numbers are not measuring the same thing. The "rg partitions finer yet wins" claim is unsupported until the real decompressed partition count (chunk size) is read from both. (The weaker claim "count doesn't track ratio / binder dead" is independently fine from DIS-24.)

The genuinely-real divergence buried under "serial in-order publish-chain" — gz publishes windows + post-processes **on the consumer** (chunk_fetcher.rs:1691 `consumer.window_publish_clean`, :1789 `consumer.window_publish_marker`) where rg does it **on the pool** off the in-order path (GzipChunkFetcher postProcessChunk / appendSubchunksToIndexes) — **is already DIS-6 item (i)**, pre-registered, NOT a new lever. DIS-6 traced the mechanism conclusively: gz's consumer LAGS its own prefetcher ~318ms while rg's stays ~0-17ms behind ⇒ gz cold-re-decodes evicted chunks (737ms = 73% of wall). DIS-25 re-describes this with a heterogeneous-core lens; it does not add a distinct fix.

### Q4 — mechanism source-plausible → SOUND but identical to DIS-6
"A slow E-core straggler head-of-line-stalls the in-order consumer." The consumer IS in-order (correct, and so is rg). A slow chunk i blocks emission of i+1.. (required for byte-order). It only IDLES workers if the 2P prefetch cache back-pressures or the straggler is cold-re-decoded — which is exactly DIS-6's eviction→cold-re-decode cascade. Plausible, but the same mechanism and the same fix surface DIS-6 found dead for prefetch-scheduling (3 reverted attempts).

### Q5 — silesia scope → FIX-NEEDED
silesia is rg's tuning corpus (chunk-size formula + prefetch tuned on it). The high-T loss magnitude is silesia-only. A squishy-variety cross-check is owed before treating the work-distribution lever as corpus-general. (DIS-24 Q5 already flags this; still open.)

## Bottom line
(B) is the right DIAGNOSIS — the high-T loss is bankable and is NOT dominantly per-core engine-W. But it is **not a new, distinct, DIS-6-clear lever**: prefetch-depth is refuted, out-of-order-publish is unfaithful (rg consumer in-order, PGR.hpp:575-628), and the finer-partitioning sub-claim rests on Total-Fetched vs finish_decode (non-comparable). The one real structural divergence (window-publish/post-process on the consumer vs off-path in rg) is DIS-6 item (i) with the **pre-registered serial-work-vs-decode-wait decompose still owed and never run.**

## Highest-value next move (not a fix)
Run the OWED DIS-6 discriminator on the critical-path chunks at T8/T16: partition gz's consumer per-chunk time into (i) SERIAL bookkeeping on the consumer (window publish + post-process: chunk_fetcher.rs:1691/1789) vs (ii) DECODE-WAIT (engine busy on the chunk). And read the REAL decompressed partition count/chunk size from both tools (not Total Fetched). That single pair of measurements decides the verdict:
- serial-work dominates ⇒ the faithful, DIS-6-distinct, NOT-engine lever is **move window-publish/post-process OFF the in-order consumer onto the pool** (cite GzipChunkFetcher postProcessChunk / appendSubchunksToIndexes) — and that is what lets rg's consumer stay 0-17ms behind. Worth a fix attempt.
- decode-wait dominates ⇒ it IS the engine on the critical chunks (the user-gated asm lever), and the lean-consumer port won't close it.

Until that runs, do NOT open a "heterogeneous work-distribution / prefetch-depth / out-of-order publish" fix — two of its three surfaces are already dead/unfaithful and the third is DIS-6's unfinished decompose.
