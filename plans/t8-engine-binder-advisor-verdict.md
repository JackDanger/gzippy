## CLAIM 1 — "charter committed a unit error (floor-vs-wall); engine floors differ ~1.74x" → **UPHELD**

Disproof attempt: if gzippy's decodeBlock secretly included a phase rg's excluded (apply_window/marker-resolve/checksum), the 0.118 "floor" would be inflated and the floor-to-floor comparison void. **Survived:** both decodeBlock spans verified to exclude applyWindow (above), so dividing each by 8 is apples-to-apples. The charter's 0.118 (gzippy floor) vs 0.130 (rg **wall**) is indeed a category mismatch — rg's own floor is ~0.068 (≈0.50/8). Caveat: rg's *printed* Theoretical-Optimal folds in applyWindow+checksum (GzipChunkFetcher.hpp:183-185), so rg's true floor is marginally >0.068; but rg runs few window-absent/applyWindow chunks (most clean→ISA-L), so the correction is small and cannot close a 1.7x gap. The unit-error finding stands.

## CLAIM 2 — "T8 binder is the ENGINE: decodeBlock 0.93s vs 0.50s = 1.86x" → **UPHELD-WITH-CAVEATS**

- **D-A (span contamination):** REFUTED as a confound — spans are symmetric (both exclude applyWindow). The 1.86x is not a window-publish artifact.
- **D-B (chunking 18 vs 17):** REFUTED as the cause. decodeBlock is a SUM over the same 212 MB decoded; it scales with bytes, not chunk count. 18/17 = 1.06 ≠ 1.86; per-chunk header is 2.5% total. Chunking cannot manufacture 1.86x.
- **Caveat (the real residual):** gzippy's decodeBlock SUM also absorbs any speculative **re-decode** of window-absent chunks. From the brief's own numbers, body 269 MB/s × ~212 MB ≈ 0.79s of the 0.93s SUM is genuine throughput; the ~0.14s remainder is header + re-decode/marker overhead. So the *pure per-byte* engine gap is ~1.58x (424 vs 269 MB/s) and the marker/re-decode architecture adds the rest up to 1.86x. Either decomposition keeps the engine (decode + its marker machinery) as the dominant term; neither is consumer-wait. Claim 2 survives; the caveat is only that "1.86x" blends raw throughput (~1.58x) with speculation/marker cost.

## CLAIM 3 — "future::get 1.25x is a minority and largely downstream of the slow engine" → **UPHELD-WITH-CAVEATS**

Disproof (D-C): could the consumer wait be the leading term once tail/pool-fill (which decodeBlock-SUM/8 hides) is restored? **Survived:** the tail-inclusive decode-phase wall is `Total Real Decode Duration` (decodeBlockEndTime−StartTime, the MAX span) = 0.139s vs 0.086s = **1.61x** — still larger than future::get's 1.25x. So even crediting tail effects fully to coordination, the decode phase out-gaps the consumer wait. future::get is also structurally partly downstream (futures resolve sooner when decode is faster). Caveats: (1) future::get is not *purely* downstream — there is a genuine in-order/first-chunk coordination floor inside it; (2) the brief decomposes the decode **pipeline**, not the full 290 ms end-to-end wall (~150 ms residual = header 24 ms + CRC/finalize/startup is undecomposed), so "engine is THE binder" is demonstrated for the decode pipeline, not proven for every ms of wall. Within the pipeline, engine > consumer-wait holds.

## D-D — consistency with the ROUND-2 PLATEAU (pure-Rust ~2.4x ISA-L in isolation) → **CORROBORATES, with a load-bearing caveat**

rapidgzip decodes window-known chunks with **ISA-L** (verified above); gzippy with pure-Rust. A decodeBlock gap of 1.86x in the mixed workload is fully consistent with a 2.4x pure-Rust-vs-ISA-L gap in isolation, diluted because gzippy's window-absent chunks face rg's *custom* (non-ISA-L) marker inflate. So D-D corroborates "engine is the binder, and it's a pure-Rust-vs-ISA-L gap." **Caveat (per [[project_engine_plateau_pure_rust]]):** the 2.4x plateau was measured on the **u16-ring** arch that rapidgzip never uses for the clean bulk; the faithful **u8-direct** clean engine was never fully built/measured. The 2.4x is therefore NOT a validated pure-Rust ceiling — it bounds the wrong engine.
